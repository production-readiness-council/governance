# Governance Model

## Purpose

The Production Readiness Council exists to produce narrow, evidence-based technical evaluations with visible review rigor and public governance clarity.

## Governing structure

### Technical Council

The Technical Council is the highest technical decision body for publication authority, governance interpretation, and governance changes unless a document delegates a narrower decision.

Responsibilities:

- approve or reject publication of verdict-bearing reports
- approve governance changes or delegated governance processes
- appoint or confirm maintainers and council members through the documented criteria
- resolve escalation when workflow disputes affect publication authority or review independence

### Working Groups

Working Groups are domain-scoped operating bodies that own proposal triage, test planning, evidence collection, and draft development for topics in their area.

Responsibilities:

- evaluate whether a proposal fits the council scope
- own the working draft and evidence plan for accepted proposals
- maintain traceable links between claims, findings, and evidence
- recommend when a draft is ready for panel review

Working Groups do not have unilateral authority to publish verdict-bearing reports.

### Report Review Panels

A Report Review Panel is formed for a specific report before publication review.

Responsibilities:

- review the draft for methodological integrity, evidence quality, and unsupported claims
- record review comments, requested changes, and conflict disclosures
- recommend whether a report is ready for publication decision, requires revision, or should stop

A panel advises publication readiness but does not override council publication authority.

### Maintainers

Maintainers are trusted contributors who manage repository workflow, documentation quality, issue hygiene, and enforcement of published process.

Responsibilities:

- keep workflow artifacts current and consistent
- moderate contribution flow and template usage
- enforce process rules in repositories
- separate administrative actions from technical verdict authority

### Operations and Administration

Operations or administrative roles manage systems, permissions, scheduling, and non-technical coordination.

Responsibilities:

- administer tooling, access, and auditability
- support the mechanics of review workflow
- avoid acting as the sole source of technical approval authority

Administrative control does not imply authority over report conclusions.

### Sponsors

Sponsors may provide resources or support, but sponsors do not control conclusions, review outcomes, publication decisions, or reviewer selection.

## Separation rules

- Proposal triage may be delegated to Working Groups.
- Publication authority remains with the Technical Council or a formally delegated council-approved process.
- Administrative access does not create verdict authority.
- Funding support does not create technical authority.
- Governance artifacts must be public and versioned unless a private disclosure exception is explicitly necessary.
