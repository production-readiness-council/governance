# Report Lifecycle

## Lifecycle states

1. Proposal Submitted
2. Triage Review
3. Accepted for Working Group Development
4. Evidence Plan Approved
5. Draft in Progress
6. Review Panel Formed
7. Panel Review Active
8. Revision Required or Publication Decision Pending
9. Published
10. Re-review Scheduled or Closed

## Gate definitions

### Gate 1: Proposal intake

A proposal enters the workflow when a GitHub proposal record exists with enough scope, problem framing, and rationale to evaluate it.

### Gate 2: Triage decision

A Working Group or delegated triage body decides whether the proposal fits scope, has a bounded question, and is practical to evaluate.

### Gate 3: Evidence-plan readiness

Before substantive drafting, the Working Group must define the test approach, expected evidence types, and known constraints.

### Gate 4: Draft readiness

A draft may move forward only when claims are linked to evidence or are clearly marked as open questions.

### Gate 5: Independent review-panel formation

No verdict-bearing report may move toward publication until a report-scoped review panel exists and conflicts are disclosed.

### Gate 6: Panel review outcome

The panel records one of three recommendations:

- ready for publication decision
- revisions required
- stop or re-scope

### Gate 7: Publication decision

A verdict-bearing report may be published only after a publication decision artifact records the approver, decision date, and rationale.

### Gate 8: Re-review decision

At publication time, the report must also record whether and when re-review is expected.

## Workflow constraints

- Proposal acceptance is not publication approval.
- Draft completion is not publication approval.
- Review-panel recommendation is not publication approval.
- Published status must not be implied before Gate 7 is satisfied.
